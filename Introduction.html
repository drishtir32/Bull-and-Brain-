🧠 Quick Stock Market Flashcards

What Is the Stock Market?

The stock market is a place where people buy and sell shares of companies. Think of it like a marketplace, but instead of fruit or clothes, you're buying parts of companies. People use it to invest and grow their money over time using apps or brokers.

📊 What Are Stocks and Shares?

A stock is a tiny piece of a company. A share is one unit of stock. Owning shares = owning part of the company. If you own stock in a company, you’re a shareholder. Shareholders may earn profits and vote on company matters.

💼 Public vs. Private Companies

Private Companies: Owned by a few (founders, investors). Not available for public to buy.
Public Companies: Stocks available to everyone. Examples: Apple, Nike, Google.
Public companies must follow rules and report earnings.

📉 What Are Dividends?

A dividend is money paid to shareholders from profits. Not all companies pay dividends. If a company pays $2 per share and you have 10 shares, you earn $20. It’s a reward for being a company owner.

💼 Core Financial Concepts

  • Market Cap = Stock price × Shares
  • Large/Mid/Small Cap refer to size
  • Price per Share = Value of 1 stock
  • Supply & Demand sets price direction
  • Bull Market = Rising prices
  • Bear Market = Falling prices
  • IPO = When company sells stock for the first time

🌍 Types of Stocks

  • Common Stock: Popular, voting rights
  • Preferred Stock: No vote, fixed dividend
  • Growth Stock: High growth, no dividend
  • Value Stock: Underpriced opportunities
  • Blue-Chip Stock: Big trusted names
  • Penny Stock: Cheap, risky
