📊 Types of Stocks

Let’s explore the three main types of stocks: Common, Preferred, and Growth stocks. Each has its own benefits and risks.

📘 Common Stock

Common stock is the most popular type of stock. When you own common stock, you:

  • Have voting rights in company decisions
  • Can receive dividends (if the company pays them)
  • Benefit when the company grows and stock prices rise

🏦 Preferred Stock

Preferred stock is a special type of stock that combines features of both stocks and bonds:

  • Usually no voting rights
  • Fixed dividend payments (like interest)
  • Paid before common shareholders if company faces bankruptcy

🚀 Growth Stock

Growth stocks are shares of companies expected to grow rapidly:

  • Often don’t pay dividends
  • Profits are reinvested to fuel company growth
  • Higher risk, but also higher reward potential
